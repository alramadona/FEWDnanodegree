Komodo dragon

The Komodo dragon, also known as the Komodo monitor, is a member of the monitor lizard family Varanidae, the largest living species of lizard.

  • Scientific Name: Varanus komodoensis
  • Maximum Length: 3 metres
  • Average Lifespan: 5 years
  • Habitat: the Indonesian islands of Komodo, Rinca, Flores, Gili Motang, and Padar

Their unusually large size has been attributed to island gigantism since no other carnivorous animals fill the niche on the islands where they live. However, recent research suggests the large size of Komodo dragons may be better understood as representative of a relict population of very large varanid lizards that once lived across Indonesia and Australia, most of which, along with another megafauna, died out after the Pleistocene (likely as a result of human activity).
